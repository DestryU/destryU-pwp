Content Strategy

My portfolio is meant to be a simple and clean design, with an influence on answering the basic questions and providing easy solutions for finding out more. The website is ideally going to be structured with two major sections, and each of those major sections will have further sub-sections as follows:

  • Splash
  • About Me
    • Who?
    • What?
    • Where?
    • Why?
  • Resources
    • Resume & Cover
    • Contact Me
    • Gallery*
    • More Examples*

All of this will be presented in the form of one very tall webpage, where users land at the top and can learn everything they need to by scrolling down.

Splash

The top of the page, the Splash, will feature a greeting, a prompt to begin scrolling down, and a quick jump button that lets users skip to the bottom.

About Me

This section is meant to be an attractive way of funneling users towards the bottom of the page, where the most important content is. Each sub-section is modeled after the 5 W's (I won't be using When). Each sub-section will be a large block-style section with a Title (who | what | where | why), a small paragraph of text that answers the sub-section question, and an associated space for an example that ties it all together.

EX: For the WHO sub section, there will be a title clearly labeled 'Who?', a paragraph of text like the one below, and a professional headshot as the example:

WHO: Hello, my name is Destry. I like books and music, I like games of both the board and video variety, and I like cartoons and toys. In a lot of ways, I'm still a kid that believes in make-pretend. But more important than what I like is what I can DO, and what I do is Interactive Design.

Very specifically, I want to include a Parallax scrolling effect throughout this section. Because I'm selling myself as a web developer, this section is meant to be the flashiest front-end I can manage; it's not just a blurb about who I am, but a practical demonstration of why my webdev and design skills are desirable.

Resources

This section is meant to be an array of options that let users find out more about me, reach out, or explore other things I've done. For this PWP, my final submittal will only really have a section featuring anchors for my contact information and resume. However, since I want to use this as my portfolio, I fully intend on expanding this section after final turn-in to include additional content, such as image galleries, links to other services, and more.
